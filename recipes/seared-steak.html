Pan-Seared Steak

Those who are familiar only with barbeque steak don't know what they are missing out on! This way of preparing steak is easy while also being surprisingly flavorful. The key is to know when to take the steak off of the heat. If you want your steak to turn out medium-rare, it is important to take it off earlier than you think, because the steak will actually still be cooking when you take it off! When it sits by itself, the meat continues cooking for several minutes. So it's very tricky to know when the right time to take it off the heat is. But trust me, it is also very rewarding!

Grab a good cast-iron pan and let's get cooking!

----- Ingredients:

  • Ribeye or Sirloin Steaks
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Butter
  • Minced garlic
  • Rosemary
    •

----- Cooking Steps:

  1. First, pat the steaks dry and season generously with salt and pepper
  2. Heat your oil on a medium-heat pan. Once the oil is very hot, place the steaks in and sear for about 4 minutes per side, including the corners.
  3. Bring the heat down and add butter, garlic and rosemary. Continue basting the steak in this sauce for 4-5 minutes.
  4. When done, take off the pan and cover for 10 minutes. Then baste one final time, slice into strips, and serve!
